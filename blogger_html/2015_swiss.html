行程介绍：

周一：早上日内瓦湖边漫步
Lake Leman介绍：Lake Leman也叫Lake Geneva，周长100多公里，东南小部分区域属于法国，大部分在瑞士境内。湖是弯月形，两个尽头分别是西南端的日内瓦和东北端Montreux。这里 最出名是Lavaux酒庄，是一个延绵二三十公里的葡萄园，是世界文化遗产。喜欢登山的，Montreux那边很多好地方（下文详述）。主要城市还有日内 瓦和洛桑。
另外，位于西南端法国中世纪小镇Yvoire也很吸引，但最好夏天去，有花很漂亮，但到了11月就比较冷清，公交也不方便，周末没船。具体公交参考下面sbb网站。离日内瓦北面不远的Nyon有个城堡，据说可以俯视湖景。城堡现在是个艺术馆，每个月第一个周日去免票。
城堡网址
http://www.chateaudenyon.ch/fr/
（下图：上面所述地方位置）

货币：欧元通用，但会亏。吃饭是一比一的。买火车票会好点，但还是会亏一些。找钱都是法郎。现在1法郎大概是0.91欧元。
插头：这个要特别注意，不是用欧洲常见的插头。瑞士插头只有瑞士和列支敦士登会用。
餐饮：消费的确很高，不吃西餐的话简简单单20欧之内可以吃饱，火车站附近Rue Chaponniere, Rue de Neuchatel会有中东菜，印度菜，东南亚菜，中餐等，算是较廉价。如果要吃正宗西餐，上档次的话要100欧。午餐的话可以去火车站附近百货大楼Manor,顶层有自助的。这里也有超市，有些外卖简餐。
住宿：青年旅馆都要将近40欧一个人。住的远一点的话，Ibis七八十欧有双人间，或者去日内瓦东侧4公里法国的Annemasse也有便宜些的，好像在火车站坐10路过去。我们住的Hotel Tor,就在大巴站对面（大巴站离火车站约步行5分钟），我们住的三人间带厨房，每晚184法郎，淡季可以到130欧左右，hotel.com预定。价格是带早餐的，送到房间吃，普普通通，有musli,牛角面包，烤方包，水果橙汁，咖啡或茶。这是很老的房子。
本地交通： 我知道在日内瓦，洛桑和Montreux,在那里住酒店都提供免费公交卡(free transport card),住宿期间可以免费使用本地交通，日内瓦更可以乘坐yellow taxi。这是往返Lake Leman短程的公交。
http://www.geneva.info/lake-boats/
除此之外，日内瓦还有免费自行车提供，详情
http://www.geneveroule.ch/
不过日内瓦本身是大城市，人多车多，我觉得不是那么适合骑车。
这真是有钱的地方，连游客坐公交骑车都可以免费。

这个好像经常不一样，现在买这个是73法郎，但必须持half fare card。之前是不用的，一天129法郎。之前还有另外一种优惠，就是一个人有half fare card,就可以带3个人一同买半价的1 day travel pass。

http://www.sbb.ch/en/travelcards-and-tickets/tickets-for-switzerland/supersaver-tickets.html
提前买半票优惠，上面说是半票数量有限，但我看不紧张，提前几分钟买也有，但必须要网上买，现场买是全票。自己打印票出来，或者用手机app,用这个票要检查护照的。
另外提醒一下，手机app买票会把信用卡信息保存的，我觉得有点不安全的说。
买全票的话，是不限班次，同一天的都可以坐，但supersaver ticket是限定班次的，错过就木有了。
有时候火车票会和景点门票配合有优惠，好像现在去Chillon城堡就是。

注意，sbb网上买火车票的时候会有city-city ticket选项，意思是除了火车外还包括两地的本地公交。但是最少在日内瓦，洛桑和Montreux,在那里住酒店都提供免费公交卡(free transport card),所以这个不太必要。
由于票种太多，我算来算去，我们这种只玩两天的，还是买单票价格最低。去程买supersaver ticket,返程不能估算时间就买全价票。本来见车票那么贵，还想自己开车，成本更低一点，难得这次有小t子司机可以用，但咨询过瑞士人，停车很困难， 而且费用很高，风景也没火车好，也就算了吧。

2015年10月31日周六 -Lavaux Vineyard远足（徒步约20公里）
Lavaux葡萄园是很出名的，延绵两三十公里，是世界文化/自然遗产，这里景色几百年没变。这个季节去是金秋，非常漂亮。 葡萄园是靠着Lake Lemen的，你坐火车大概从洛桑开始就可以看到，直到Montreux。
如果你体力有限，可以走这段Terrasses de Lavaux，11公里。这段路的葡萄园是最密集的，是当地旅游局推荐线路。我建议从St Saphorin走路（也可以做火车，走路也就几公里，不过很陡）上Chexbrex,然后再下来Rivaz再去Lutry。从Chexbrex走下来一 大遍葡萄园非常非常漂亮的说。上面说的几个地方都有火车站的。

最 后，在这简单科普一下，我家附近也有葡萄园，也是斜坡，这边也是，所以引起我的好奇，葡萄园都在斜坡上的吗？是的，首先最好的斜坡是朝南，这里正是。因为 太阳过中天时是朝南的，所以朝南的斜坡可以吸收更多阳光（过中天是天文词汇，不懂自行百度）。另外，太阳光线和地面不是呈90度的，具体角度根据地理纬度 和太阳位于黄道位置，总之呈90度是光线最强。而葡萄园长在斜坡上是可以90度接收阳光。还有，斜坡就是不会积水，这有利生长。有兴趣的参考

2015年11月1日周日 - Chillon城堡及日内瓦市内
日内瓦市内没多大可逛，简单说一下。
CERN
这 是全球最大粒子物理实验室，位于日内瓦。没有听过的大概也听过希格斯玻色子，Higgs Boson,也称“上帝粒子”，2013年拿了诺贝尔物理学奖的。而发现Higgs Boson的就是CERN。如果你对CERN有兴趣，他们有免费导游团，但人很多，提前两周预约，需要抢位。另外，如果你想在CERN外面看，他们也有路 线规划，骑车玩的。具体参考
https://outreach.web.cern.ch/outreach/
Lake Leman南面有老城。北面有联合国，也提供导游团，详情参考
http://www.unog.ch/
Chillon城堡 - 位于Montreux以东4公里，靠近湖边，大概在Lake Leman拐弯处，中世纪城堡，具体历史不详。成人门票12法郎，学生10.5，在Montrex住可能有优惠，详见官网。
http://www.chillon.ch/en/index.cfm​
去 城堡可以从日内瓦火车直达,坐火车到Veytaux-Chillon,车程约1.5小时。下车往南走大概400米就到城堡。还可以在Montreux坐公 交201，这个直接到城堡门口。出火车站按指示走一小段路，下楼梯，然后看到Escaliers de la Gare这个站就是了。

最后是瑞士爬山狂人的爬山推荐，在Montreux那边，适合低水平人士
Option 1:

Geneva - Montreux by train, then Montreux - Rocher de Naye by train. Once up there, you can hike to the summit (10 min), and hike a bit more around the cliff (or further away from the cliff) and check the mountain flower garden (assuming it is still open in that season). This will work no matter what the weather is, even if there is snow. But if there is snow, then I would suggest not to wander too far away from the station (going to the summit will be always safe - maybe slippery). Depending on the mood/fitness ability/adventure spirit/time/weather, you can hike down a bit to the next station, but the trail involves either going through a cave (make sure to bring torches (at least one each + one backup, + spare batteries), or down a fairly steep slope (with the trail well marked, but you do NOT want to do that during/after rain, or if there is snow or ice on the ground !). Or simply take the train back from the top.

Around Montreux, should the weather be terrible, you can go check out Chillon's castle, built on the lake itself, or simply hike around the place (the lakeside is rather scenic).

The train up the mountain is NOT cheap: ~70 CHF return for adults ; http://www.goldenpass.ch/en/goldenpass/offer/view?id=12

Option 2:

Train to Sion, then take a bus to Derborance, along a crazy, narrow and steep mountain road. make sure to sit on the LEFT side of the bus on the way up, and on the RIGHT side of the bus on the way down. I'd suggest trying to sleep somewhere in Derborance (some mountain hostel should still be open), and take one day to explore the area which is really beautiful with lakes and fancy forests with pine trees (which may have great colors, depending on the weather). If the weather is good, you can hike up to the Anzeide plateau, another beautiful spot with lots of wildlife (marmote, chamoix, etc ...), and a perfect and gentle day excursion along well-marked trails. As usual, only to be attempted without snow - the locals will be able to tell you how good/bad things are if you are unsure. Even if the weather is terrible, you might still have a good time going up there and spending the night, as you hear thunder echoing on the mountains and rock falling down the cliffs.

Option 1 is easier and with the best view over the lake, but Option 2 can be much more scenic in terms of mountain experience and crazy winding roads. Happy to help further with tickets and so on. You don't need to buy any train/bus ticket in advance, but it is imperative to check the timetables to make sure you actually have a train to catch, and when you need to be at the different stations.

Here is a link to the timetable to go to Derborance : https://www.postauto.ch/fr/timetable?loc[origin][name]=Geneva&loc[origin][code]=&loc[destination][name]=Derborence&loc[destination][code]=&via[name]=&via[code]=&tm[time][date]=09.10.2015&tm[time][time]=05%3A20&tm[mode]=1&vh[all]=all&vh[2]=2&vh[4]=4&vh[1]=1&vh[8]=8&vh[32]=32&vh[16]=16

It looks like the trip takes 3 h from Geneva, and only runs on Saturdays and Sundays, and costs 70 CHF one way. So, you could go up on Saturday, and arrive there at 11am. And leave on the Sunday at 4:40pm, arriving in Geneva around 8:00pm. Assuming you are there on a weekend ?

For your travel, there is something called the "Swiss travel Pass". Basically, a ticket that gives you access to almost all the bus, trains and boats in Switzerland. They have a 3-day option for 210 CHF second class for adults (180 for < 26 yrs) which is worth considering.

http://www.swiss-pass.ch/en/swiss-pass/?gclid=CNqLlviztMgCFQ-VvQodBFkPhQ

The map of connection (http://www.stc.ch/download/pdf/uebersichtskarte_en.pdf) also can give you some ideas of places you can reach like that. Zermatt is one of the most known place in Switzerland (and likely very expensive to sleep at), but Saas Fee is also beautiful.

There is a big Dam at Dixence which you can reach by bus, as well as in Mauvoisin. Note that the train to les Rochers de Naye (option 1) is NOT covered by the Swiss Pass (you only get 50% off).
